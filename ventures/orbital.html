Exploring the Future of AI Agents in Complex Environments

Orbital is a pioneering research, development, and implementation initiative focused on AI agents for complex systems.

The project is dedicated to exploring, designing, and deploying production-grade artificial intelligence agents within real-world, high-complexity environments. Its mission is not theoretical research, but applied innovation: building, operating, and refining AI agent systems that solve tangible problems inside mature products and organizations.

At its core is the investigation and implementation of AI agents together with their "harnesses" — the tooling, orchestration layers, meta-structures, and operational frameworks required for agents to perform reliably, safely, and effectively in production systems.

An Applied AI Lab for Production Systems

The project operates as an applied AI lab, tightly integrated with existing companies such as Numia, CloudClerk, MiFisio, and other ventures within the group. These companies act as living laboratories, where AI agent systems are designed and implemented end to end, deployed in real production environments, and continuously evaluated, iterated, and improved.

  • Research and experimentation on agent architectures, orchestration patterns, and multi-agent systems
  • Design and implementation of AI agent harnesses, including tool use, memory, control flows, evaluation loops, and safety constraints
  • Definition of best practices for building, operating, and scaling AI agent systems in complex products
  • Knowledge sharing, ensuring that learnings and patterns are documented and made reusable for other teams and organizations

Services and Enablement

Beyond internal implementation, the team also works directly with external organizations, offering:

  • Audits of existing AI or automation systems
  • Consulting and advisory services for teams looking to adopt AI agents in their products or operations
  • Hands-on training and enablement, helping internal teams understand, operate, and extend agent-based systems
  • Implementation support, adapting internally-developed systems to specific business and technical contexts

A key principle is leading by example: every system, pattern, or recommendation shared externally is first validated in production within our own companies.

How It Started

The project emerged from the recognition that many organizations struggle to move AI agents from experimentation into reliable, production-ready systems. While tooling and models evolve rapidly, the real challenge lies in system design, integration, and operational discipline.

Backed by the Numia team — recognized for their expertise in large-scale systems, data-intensive platforms, and applied innovation — it was created to bridge this gap. The focus is not only on what agents can do, but on how they should be built, governed, and operated inside complex organizations.

The initiative is intentionally structured as a continuous, open-ended project, where knowledge compounds over time and is actively shared. By combining real production usage, research-driven iteration, and transparent knowledge transfer, it aims to push forward the practical adoption of AI agents across industries.
